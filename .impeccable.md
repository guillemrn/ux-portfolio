## Design Context

### Users
Potential employers, clients, and design peers looking to evaluate UX design and technical implementation skills. The context is high-stakes professional review where clarity, attention to detail, and a unique point of view are paramount.

### Brand Personality
Editorial, Precise, Elegant. The goal is to evoke confidence and sophistication through a blend of traditional serif typography and modern web capabilities.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual Tone**: Minimalist editorial. High reliance on whitespace and strong typographic hierarchy.
- **Theme**: Light-themed but with a warm, "paper-like" look using Cream and Deep Dark Green. No pure blacks or whites unless necessary for contrast.
- **References**: High-end architecture and fashion portfolios.
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS landing pages, cluttered "noisy" designs, over-saturated color palettes.

### Design Principles
1. **Editorial Clarity**: Prioritize content readability and visual hierarchy above all else. Use whitespace as a functional element.
2. **Purposeful Motion**: Leverage `framer-motion` and `lenis` for smooth, non-intrusive transitions that guide the user's attention.
3. **Natural Sophistication**: Use the palette of Cream (`#F7F2E8`), Deep Dark Green (`#052E1C`), and Accent Green (`#29D067`) to create a refined, unique atmosphere.
4. **Attention to Micro-Detail**: Ensure interactions like the custom cursor and selection states feel intentional and reinforce the premium brand identity.
